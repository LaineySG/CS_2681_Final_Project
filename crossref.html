Minimum Requirement Features

2.2.1 HTML5

  1. Appropriate HTML5 tags, including use of HTML5 structural tags, <a>, <p>, <blockquote>, <ol>, <ul>, and <dl>, to name a few
  2. Inline images both static and clickable
  3. Use of appropriate special characters
  4. Use of appropriate <meta> tags
  5. A footer on the home page indicating when the website was last updated or modified

2.2.2 CSS3

  1. Use CSS3 to format the appearance of paragraphs, lists, headers, background images, tables, and forms
  2. Use CSS3 contextual selectors (e1, e2, e3; e f; e > f; e + f) and at least two attribute selectors, excluding class and id attribute selectors
  3. Use of CSS3 styles for color(RGBA & HSLA)
  4. Use of dynamic pseudo-classes to highlight hyperlinks states of link, visited, active, hover; focus and two structural pseudo-classes: first-of-type and last-of-type
  5. Use of CSS3 background styles (background size, background-clip, background-origin, padding, margins, borders, use of an appropriate border style, use of rounded corners)
  6. Your home page should have a drop cap created with CSS3.
  7. Define document content with the class and id properties and create styles for them.
  8. Use each of the following page layout techniques:
    1. Grid
    2. Flex
    3. Float
    4. Positioning
    (Note: Do not mix multiple page layout techniques on a single page.) The use of framework for layout is discouraged due to too many unknown factors.
  9. Use of CSS3 text and box shadows
  10. Use of CSS3 transform rotate feature
  11. Use of CSS3 opacity applied to two images to appear semi-transparent

2.2.3 Web Form

  1. Use of appropriate form elements including some of the new HTML5 form elements
  2. Apply appropriate styles to web forms.
  3. Apply inline form validation by using the focus pseudo-class to appropriate form elements.

2.2.4 Multimedia

  1. Use sound and video somewhere on your site. Ensure these are of short duration, preferably about 30 seconds.
  2. Use of transitions and animations

2.2.5 JavaScript

  1. Use of variables, arithmetic, comparison and logical operators, subroutines, functions, arrays, conditional statements, conditional operators, and running time commands with system clock
  2. Use of system date (example: clock, countdown timer to an event) on your website
  3. Validating form input by using appropriate JavaScript functions
  4. Use of JavaScript alerts for validation purposes.

2.2.6 Responsive Design

  1. Your website design should include responsive design implementation using concepts covered in this course that would accommodate desktop, tablet, and mobile designs, including positioning, grid, and flex layouts.
  2. The use of contextual selectors to control sub level menus is required, including a navicon image for mobile design.
  3. Images should also be responsive for different screen sizes and devices.

2.3 Documentation

Include the following in PDF format:
  1. Front page (with your name, student id, course name, and date)
  2. A paragraph entailing the business statement in about 100 words
  3. Project objectives and a project description stated clearly
  4. A brief explanation of how the business would benefit from your design, in about 100 words
  5. Any other appropriate relevant documentation pertaining to your site that you wish to include
  6. Documentation of the flow of Web Pages. It is important for you to develop a flow of the website. The flow essentially refers to how a user will navigate through the site—how the pages will link to each other. Provide written instructions on overall navigation of the business website. You can provide visuals as well but they are not required.

2.4 Demonstration Evaluation Form

  • Design a short evaluation form that the potential business would complete.
  • The evaluation form should include opportunities for feedback on your design, its usefulness, and the business personnel’s overall impression of your design
  • Questions could include those that ask the person to rate the website elements on a scale of 1 to 5. Ensure to ask if they have any other comments.
